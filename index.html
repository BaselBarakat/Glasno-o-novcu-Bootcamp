Bootcamp schedule

Glasno o novcu – 5-Day Bootcamp

2 sessions per day · 6 project components · full team formation to final pitch

Day 1
People, personalities & first connections
Session 1 · Morning
Introductions & personality profiling
Each participant introduces themselves to the group, sharing their background, skills, and what brought them to the campaign. A structured personality assessment follows to map individual working styles and strengths.
Activities
2-minute self-introduction per participant: name, background, one skill, one thing they want to learn
DISC or MBTI-style personality assessment (online or printed) with live scoring
Group debrief: facilitator maps results on a visual board, highlighting the range of profiles in the room
Discussion: how do different personality types contribute to digital campaign work, design, and development?
Output: Participant profile wall — names, skills, and personality type visible throughout the bootcamp
Session 2 · Afternoon
Team building activity & team formation
A structured collaborative challenge designed to reveal natural leadership, creativity, communication, and problem-solving styles — before teams are formally assigned. Ends with official team formation and a first team agreement.
Activities
Team challenge: "Financial myth or fact?" — groups race to correctly categorise 20 finance statements using only collective knowledge, no phones
Facilitator observes collaboration patterns and informal roles during the challenge
Facilitated reflection: what roles did people naturally take? How does this map to project needs?
Official team formation based on personality balance and skill mix; teams receive their assigned project component
Teams draft a one-page team agreement: communication norms, decision-making, accountability
Output: Formed teams with assigned components + signed team agreement per group
Day 2
Understanding the landscape & finding opportunities
Session 1 · Morning
Market research & problem definition
Teams deep-dive into the current financial literacy landscape — what exists, who it reaches, and where it fails. Guided research frames the specific problem "Glasno o novcu" is built to solve, grounding all future creative decisions in evidence.
Activities
Facilitated overview: financial literacy rates in the region, existing campaigns, benchmark platforms internationally
Competitive audit: each team analyses 2–3 comparable campaigns or platforms (web, social, quiz-based) and presents key findings
Audience mapping: who are the primary users of each project component — demographics, digital habits, knowledge gaps
Problem statement workshop: teams draft a crisp "How might we…" problem statement specific to their component
Output: One-page market brief + audience persona + "How might we" problem statement per team
Session 2 · Afternoon
Brainstorming & opportunity mapping
Building on the morning's research, teams move from analysis to ideation. Structured brainstorming methods push past obvious solutions, with cross-pollination between teams to spark unexpected combinations and fresh angles.
Activities
Crazy 8s: each participant sketches 8 rough ideas in 8 minutes for their component
Gallery walk: teams post ideas on the wall and all participants add dot votes and sticky-note reactions
Cross-team "idea swap": one member rotates to another team's wall and identifies synergies or borrowed ideas
Opportunity ranking matrix: each team places top ideas on an impact vs. feasibility grid to identify their strongest 2–3 directions
Output: Ranked opportunity map with 2–3 shortlisted concept directions per team
Day 3
Pitching direction & planning the build
Session 1 · Morning
Team pitch & role assignment
Each team pitches their chosen concept direction to the full group and a panel of facilitators. Structured feedback sharpens the concept before teams lock in their approach and assign individual roles for the build phase.
Activities
5-minute team pitch per group: problem, proposed solution, target audience, and what success looks like
Structured Q&A (5 min per team): panel probes assumptions, feasibility, and differentiation
Facilitator-led feedback round: one thing that is strong, one risk to address, one open question
Role mapping workshop: teams assign Lead, Deputy, and Contributor roles across design, content, tech, and strategy tracks based on skills and personality profiles from Day 1
Output: Approved concept direction + role matrix + individual task ownership list
Session 2 · Afternoon
Architecture sprint & low-fidelity prototyping
With roles set and concepts approved, teams shift into planning mode — mapping out the structure of their component and creating the first tangible artefacts. Wireframes, content outlines, or storyboards become the shared language for the build day ahead.
Activities
Information architecture or content mapping: teams sketch the structure, user flow, or narrative arc of their component
Low-fidelity prototyping: web/quiz teams wireframe key screens; influencer teams outline a content brief; 3D/design teams sketch visual concepts
Peer review between teams: swap drafts and give one round of structured written feedback
Build-day planning: teams produce a sprint board with tasks, owners, and time estimates for Day 4
Output: Low-fidelity prototype or structural outline + sprint board ready for Day 4
Day 4
Deep build & cross-team integration
Session 1 · Morning
Focused build sprint
The core production session of the bootcamp. Teams work autonomously on their assigned component, translating plans and prototypes into real, demo-ready outputs. Facilitators rotate between teams acting as consultants, not directors.
Activities
Web platform team: develop key pages and content structure for the "Glasno o novcu" hub; draft at least two educational articles
Quiz team: build the interactive prize quiz — question set, scoring logic, and UX flow
Digital ads team: produce campaign concept, targeting strategy, and two ad creative executions
Influencer team: finalise the collaboration brief, content guidelines, and proposed channel strategy
3D/visualisation team: produce one complete interactive concept or animated explainer demonstrating a financial topic
Output: Component draft at 70–80% completion, ready for review and integration
Session 2 · Afternoon
Integration, user testing & iteration
Teams step out of their silos to test how individual components connect into one coherent campaign. Peer-led user testing surfaces friction and gaps; a focused iteration cycle brings outputs to presentation standard.
Activities
Integration workshop: teams present their output to the adjacent component team and identify how they connect (e.g. how the quiz links to the web platform; how 3D assets appear in ads)
User testing pairs: one person from each team acts as a "user" and attempts to interact with another team's output, noting confusion points
Feedback synthesis: teams compile testing notes into a prioritised list of issues — critical, nice-to-have, out-of-scope
Iteration sprint: 45 minutes to address critical issues; facilitators help teams triage scope vs. time
Output: Tested and refined component at presentation quality + integration map showing cross-component connections
Day 5
Final presentations & looking forward
Session 1 · Morning
Presentation prep & full dress rehearsal
Teams prepare their final presentations with a focus on storytelling, clarity, and demonstrating real value to a campaign stakeholder audience. A full dress rehearsal with timed slots and live feedback gives every team a chance to sharpen before the real thing.
Activities
Storytelling framework: facilitator walks through the "problem → insight → solution → proof → ask" structure for presenting campaign components
Teams prepare a 10-minute presentation deck covering: the problem they addressed, their solution, key design decisions, and what the component delivers for the wider campaign
Dress rehearsal: each team presents in full with a timer; peers provide structured written feedback on clarity, confidence, and content
Final polish: 30-minute targeted revision window based on rehearsal notes
Output: Polished 10-minute presentation per team + complete demo-ready component
Session 2 · Afternoon
Final presentations, conclusions & future work
Each team delivers their final presentation to the full group and facilitators. The bootcamp closes with a structured reflection on what was built, what was learned, and the concrete next steps each component needs to move from prototype to live campaign.
Activities
Final presentations: each team delivers their 10-minute pitch with live demo; facilitators and peers score on creativity, feasibility, campaign alignment, and presentation quality
Panel Q&A: 5 minutes of questions per team from the room
Facilitator summary: key themes, standout ideas, and moments of unexpected cross-component synergy observed across the 5 days
Future roadmap workshop: each team maps their component's immediate next steps (next 2 weeks), medium-term milestones (1–3 months), and open questions to resolve before launch
Closing reflection: individual written response — one thing learned, one thing to carry forward, one message for the next cohort
Output: Scored presentations + roadmap document per component + personal reflection record for each participant
